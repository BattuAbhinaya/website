MY FAVOURITE PERSON

HARDIK PANDYA

Hardik Pandya is a prominent Indian international cricketer, celebrated for his dynamic all-round abilities as a right-handed middle-order batsman and a fast-medium bowler. Born on October 11, 1993, in Surat, Gujarat, Pandya overcame humble beginnings—his family relocated to Vadodara to provide better cricket training for him and his brother, Krunal, who is also a professional cricketer. Hardik’s early cricket development took place at Kiran More’s academy, and he left school after ninth grade to focus entirely on the sport. Pandya made his mark in domestic cricket with Baroda and quickly rose through the ranks, debuting in the Indian Premier League (IPL) with Mumbai Indians in 2015. He played a crucial role in the franchise’s title wins and later captained Gujarat Titans to a championship in their inaugural season in 2022, before returning to Mumbai Indians as captain. Known for his aggressive batting, ability to clear boundaries with ease, and his knack for taking wickets at crucial moments, Pandya has become one of India’s most valuable players in white-ball cricket. Internationally, Pandya debuted for India in 2016 and has since contributed to significant victories, including being vice-captain during India’s 2024 T20 World Cup triumph. His career has not been without controversy, but his resilience and match-winning performances have solidified his reputation as a game-changer and a modern cricketing icon.

SHREYAS IYER

Shreyas Iyer is a distinguished Indian international cricketer, recognized for his role as a right-handed middle-order batter. Born on December 6, 1994, in Mumbai, Iyer’s cricketing journey began in the city’s famed maidans and was nurtured by coach Pravin Amre, who first spotted his talent during his school days. His family, originally from Kerala, moved to Mumbai, which provided him with greater opportunities to pursue cricket. Iyer’s domestic career took off with Mumbai, where he was the leading run-scorer in the 2014-15 Ranji Trophy season. His breakthrough came in the 2015 Indian Premier League (IPL) when the Delhi Daredevils (now Delhi Capitals) acquired him for ₹2.6 crore, making him the highest-earning uncapped player at the time. He scored 439 runs in his debut season and won the IPL Emerging Player of the Year award. Over the years, Iyer became a key player and captain for Delhi, later leading Kolkata Knight Riders and, from 2025, captaining Punjab Kings. Internationally, Iyer debuted in T20Is and ODIs in 2017 and made a memorable Test debut in 2021, becoming the first Indian to score a century and a fifty in his maiden Test match. He played pivotal roles in India’s campaigns, including a century in the 2023 World Cup semi-final and being part of the squads that won the 2023 Asia Cup and the 2025 Champions Trophy. Known for his aggressive batting and sharp fielding, Iyer is celebrated as one of India’s modern cricketing stars.

SHUBMAN Gill

Shubman Gill, born on September 8, 1999, is a prominent Indian international cricketer known for his elegant right-handed batting style. He plays across all formats for the India national team and currently captains the Test side, while also serving as the vice-captain in ODIs and having captained the T20I team. Domestically, he represents Punjab and leads the Gujarat Titans in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Gill made his international debut in January 2019 against New Zealand and quickly established himself as a prolific run-scorer. He holds the record for being the fastest player to reach 2000 ODI runs in just 38 innings and 2500 runs in 50 innings. At 23, he became the youngest Indian to score a double century in ODIs. His consistent performances were instrumental in India’s victory in the 2025 ICC Champions Trophy, where he also served as vice-captain. Before his senior international career, Gill shone in the Under-19 circuit, notably scoring 372 runs at an average of 124.00 in the 2018 Under-19 World Cup, earning the Player of the Tournament award and playing a key role in India’s title win. His unbeaten 102 in the semi-final against Pakistan U-19 received praise from cricket legends like Rahul Dravid and Sachin Tendulkar. Known for his calm demeanor and leadership qualities, Gill is viewed as a future cornerstone of Indian cricket leadership, especially as the team transitions after the era of veterans like Virat Kohli and Rohit Sharma. His blend of technical skill and quiet confidence positions him as a promising captain and a vital player for India’s cricketing future.

MS DHONI

SMahendra Singh Dhoni, born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, is one of the most celebrated cricketers in Indian and world cricket history. Renowned as a right-handed batsman and wicket-keeper, Dhoni is widely regarded as one of the greatest wicket-keeper batsmen and captains in the sport. He captained India in all formats from 2007 to 2017 (limited overs) and 2008 to 2014 (Tests), becoming the most successful Indian captain and the only one to win all three major ICC limited-overs tournaments: the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2011 Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. Dhoni's calm and composed leadership earned him the nickname "Captain Cool." Under his captaincy, India also won the Asia Cup thrice (2010, 2016, 2018) and reached the pinnacle of ICC Test rankings. He is known for his aggressive yet calculated batting style and exceptional wicket-keeping skills, holding records for most dismissals by an Indian wicket-keeper and most stumpings in international cricket In the Indian Premier League (IPL), Dhoni has been synonymous with the Chennai Super Kings (CSK), leading them to five IPL titles and two Champions League Twenty20 titles. He holds records for most IPL appearances and has been pivotal in CSK’s dominance since the league’s inception in 2008. Dhoni scored over 17,000 international runs, including 10,773 in ODIs at an average above 50, with 16 Test centuries and 10 ODI hundreds. He retired from Test cricket in 2014 and from international limited-overs cricket in 2020 but continues to play in the IPL. In 2025, Dhoni was inducted into the ICC Hall of Fame, cementing his legacy as a cricketing legend.

KRUNAL PANDYA

Krunal Pandya is an Indian cricketer known for his all-round abilities—batting left-handed and bowling left-arm orthodox spin. Born on March 24, 1991, in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, Krunal is the elder brother of fellow Indian cricketer Hardik Pandya. His rise to fame began in the Indian Premier League (IPL) with the Mumbai Indians, where he played a key role in their multiple title wins, including earning the Player of the Match award in the 2017 IPL final. He made his international debut for India in a T20I against the West Indies in 2018 and later impressed in his ODI debut against England in 2021, scoring a record-breaking 58* off just 31 balls—the fastest half-century by an ODI debutant After several successful seasons with Mumbai, Krunal was picked by the Lucknow Super Giants in 2022, where he also served as vice-captain. In 2025, he joined Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB), making a significant impact. In the IPL 2025 final, Krunal delivered a match-winning performance, taking 2 wickets for just 17 runs, helping RCB win their maiden IPL title. With this, he became the only player to win the Player of the Match award in two different IPL finals. Off the field, Krunal is known for his calm demeanor and strong family values. He is married to Pankhuri Sharma, and the couple has two sons. Through consistency, passion, and crucial performances under pressure, Krunal Pandya has carved a respected place in Indian cricket.
